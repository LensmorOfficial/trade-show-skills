# On-Site Skills

Skills for use during a trade show — staff preparation, lead qualification, and competitive intelligence capture on the show floor.

## Available Skills

### [booth-script-generator](../booth-script-generator/)

**Stage**: On-Site · **Category**: Lead Qualification

Generate booth conversation scripts by visitor type so staff have distinct opening lines, qualification questions, and CTAs for cold walk-ups, warm leads, competitor customers, and current customers.

**When to use**: 1-2 weeks before the show for training, and again during setup if the team needs a quick refresher.

---

### [badge-qualifier](../badge-qualifier/)

**Stage**: On-Site · **Category**: Lead Qualification

Transform booth conversation notes, badge scans, or voice transcripts into structured lead records with tier, fit, urgency, and next-step guidance.

**When to use**: During the show day after key conversations, or in a batch at the end of the day before context fades.

---

### [competitor-radar](../competitor-radar/)

**Stage**: On-Site · **Category**: Competitive Intelligence

Turn show-floor observations, brochure text, pricing clues, and overheard claims into structured competitor intel with source tagging.

**When to use**: After booth visits, brochure pickups, or at the end of the day to consolidate field notes before the details fade.

---

## Typical On-Site Workflow

```text
Before doors open
  -> booth-script-generator
  -> brief staff on openings, qualification paths, and CTAs

During the show
  -> badge-qualifier
  -> capture lead quality while conversations are still fresh
  -> competitor-radar
  -> log competitor claims with [OBS] / [INF] / [HEARD]

End of day
  -> badge-qualifier (batch)
  -> competitor-radar (consolidation)
  -> prepare clean lead tiers and intel notes for post-show follow-up
```

## Practical Notes

- Use `booth-script-generator` before the team memorizes bad habits. It is a training aid, not just a content generator.
- Use `badge-qualifier` in real time for Hot leads, and in batch for low-context badge scans.
- Use `competitor-radar` whenever a claim could influence pricing, positioning, or product messaging after the show.
